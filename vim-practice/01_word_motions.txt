VIM PRACTICE — Word Motions
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Motions: w  b  e  W  B  E  ge  gE

  w   — forward to start of next word
  b   — backward to start of current/previous word
  e   — forward to end of current/next word
  ge  — backward to end of previous word
  W/B/E — same but WORD (whitespace-delimited, ignores punctuation)

Solutions are folded. Use  za  to toggle, zM to close all, zR to open all.
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════


EXERCISE 1 — Count your jumps
──────────────────────────────
Cursor starts at [*]. How many  w  presses to reach "seven"?

  [*]one two three four five six seven eight nine ten

Before peeking: guess the number, then verify.

▶ SOLUTION {{{
  6w — "one" is word 0 (cursor on it), six w presses land on "seven"
  Also valid: 6W (same result; no punctuation here)
  Faster: /seven<CR> — but that's a search, not a word motion
}}}


EXERCISE 2 — Backward navigation
──────────────────────────────────
Cursor starts at [*]. Reach "start" in the fewest keystrokes.

  start middle-of middle end [*]here

▶ SOLUTION {{{
  3b  — lands on "start"  (b treats hyphen as word boundary)
  2B  — lands on "start"  (B treats whole "middle-of" as one WORD — only 2 jumps)
  Winner: 2B (fewer keystrokes when words have punctuation/hyphens)
}}}


EXERCISE 3 — End-of-word motion
─────────────────────────────────
Cursor starts at [*] (on the 'f' of "fix"). Delete to end of word without
entering insert mode and without deleting the space after the word.

  Please [*]fix this sentence carefully.

▶ SOLUTION {{{
  de  — deletes to end of current word ("fix"), leaves the space
  vs dw — deletes "fix " (word + trailing space) — leaves no space, slightly wrong
  de is the right tool here
}}}


EXERCISE 4 — Mixed word sizes
───────────────────────────────
Cursor starts at [*] (on "var"). Reach "complicated" using the fewest total keystrokes.
The line has mixed word/WORD boundaries.

  [*]var myObject.field = someVeryLong_and.complicated + 1;

▶ SOLUTION {{{
  Using w: 7w  (var → myObject → . → field → = → someVeryLong_and → . → complicated)
           wait — let's count: myObject(1) .(2) field(3) =(4) someVeryLong_and(5) .(6) complicated(7) = 7w ✓
  Using W: 4W  (var → myObject.field → = → someVeryLong_and.complicated) = 4W ✓
  Winner: 4W — use W/B when tokens have punctuation and you want to skip over it
}}}


EXERCISE 5 — ge and gE
────────────────────────
Cursor starts at [*] (on "end"). Navigate backward to the end of "second"
(land on the 'd') using a single motion.

  first second third [*]end

▶ SOLUTION {{{
  ge  — moves backward to end of previous word (lands on 'd' of "third")
  2ge — moves to end of "second" (two words back)
  Alternative: bb then e — two motions, less elegant
  Note: ge is underused; it's the backward partner of e
}}}


EXERCISE 6 — Real edit: swap word order
─────────────────────────────────────────
Transform this line (cursor at [*]) so "world" comes before "hello":

  [*]hello world

Target: world hello

▶ SOLUTION {{{
  Method A (cut and paste):
    deep  — deletes "hello " (de deletes word, p pastes — but ordering is tricky)

  Method B (clearest):
    dawwP  — d(aw) deletes "hello " with space, w moves to "world", P pastes before cursor
    Result: "world hello" ✓

  Method C (visual):
    vawwP — same concept with visual select first

  Breakdown of dawwP:
    daw — delete "hello" + surrounding space → clipboard: "hello ", cursor on "world"
    w   — move past "world" to space (optional depending on cursor position after daw)
    P   — paste before current word
    Adjust spacing as needed
}}}
